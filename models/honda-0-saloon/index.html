Overview: Honda 0 Series Saloon

Honda's flagship EV, in low-slung sedan form.

The 2026 Honda 0 Series Saloon is Honda's electric flagship — a sleek, low-slung four-door that prioritizes aerodynamics and driver focus over the SUV stance. With up to 320 miles of EPA-estimated range, an ultra-low coefficient of drag, and the same 800V platform and ASIMOS software as the 0 Series SUV, the Saloon is Honda's statement about what an EV can be. The driving position is low and sporty, the visibility through the steeply raked windshield is excellent, and the rear seat is roomier than the silhouette suggests.

How It Compares to Competitors

The 0 Series Saloon competes with the Tesla Model 3, Hyundai Ioniq 6, and Lucid Air Pure. The Honda is more spacious than the Model 3, more refined than the Ioniq 6, and far more affordable than the Lucid. Tesla still wins on Supercharger access and software polish.

Expert Review

The 0 Series Saloon is the most exciting Honda sedan in decades. The driving position alone is worth the price of admission for enthusiasts who've been priced out of luxury sedans. We averaged 4.0 mi/kWh on a mixed 500-mile route — better than the Model 3 we tested under similar conditions.
